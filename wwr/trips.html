Great Marañon

The Marañon River is a major tributary of the Amazon River, flowing from the center of Perú in a northwest direction through the Andes to turn east in the north, then turning into the Ucayali River which joins to form the Amazon River.

Amazing Urubamba

The Urubamba river runs from the high Andean ranges down through the Sacred Valley of the Incas, past Machupicchu and into the jungle. It has some exciting white water depending on the section of the river being done and the time of the year.

Majestic Apurimac

The Apurimac River, originating from the Andean Mountains of Peru, is considered the most immediate source of the mighty Amazon River. This magnificent river boasts several sections perfect for adrenaline-fueled rafting adventures. These sections are characterized by varying degrees of difficulty, ranging from calm and gentle stretches to thrilling and challenging rapids that will leave you breathless. Rafting along the Apurimac River allows you to experience the stunning natural beauty of the surrounding landscape and witness the diverse wildlife within its ecosystem.

General Information:

The Marañón, Urubamba, and Apurímac rivers are famous for their exciting rafting experiences. These rivers offer a thrilling adventure to those who seek to navigate through their turbulent waters and rapids while being surrounded by stunning natural landscapes. The rivers pass through a variety of terrains, from deep canyons to lush jungles, giving adventurers an exhilarating and challenging experience. The clear and wild waters of these rivers provide the perfect blend of adrenaline and natural beauty, making them ideal destinations for those searching for an unforgettable water adventure.
